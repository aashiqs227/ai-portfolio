Process

How I Work with AI

AI is a cognitive tool, not a magic button. My workflow treats it as a system — structured inputs produce structured outputs.

1

Context First

Prompts are drafted in a Notes app — full situation, task definition, and constraints loaded before the AI sees anything. No lazy inputs.

2

Smartest Models

Always use the most capable model available. Older or free-tier models produce outputs that feel years behind. The gap compounds.

3

Expertise Layer

AI supplements, it doesn't replace. Domain knowledge shapes the prompt, evaluates the output, and makes the final call. That's where above-average happens.

Philosophy

On Leverage

Anyone not using AI — or not using the most capable models of the time — will fall behind. That dynamic is now best understood as leverage. Those who adapt and implement AI into their workflows gain compounding advantages over those who don't. In a marketing environment, this leverage shows up across every layer: content creation, campaign planning, automating repetitive operations, and increasingly, the complete management and optimization of paid campaigns. The question isn't whether to use AI — it's how fast you can build the systems around it.
